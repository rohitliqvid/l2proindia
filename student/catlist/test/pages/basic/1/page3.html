Features of Intellectual Property – Intangible Property
 
 
 

It’s important to know that IPRs protect intangible property. Creations of the mind are intangible. For example, goods like cars can be traded and recalled. Ideas once conveyed, i.e. knowledge, cannot be taken back. Hence, IP law provides innovators and creators with the legal “right to exclude ” others from using or practicing a patent, copyright, or other forms of IP.
